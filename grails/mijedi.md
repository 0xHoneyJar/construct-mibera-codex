## Cultural Context

In Star Wars expanded lore, orange lightsabers are among the rarest color variants, absent from the original trilogy, almost invisible in the prequel era, and emerging primarily in video games and later canon materials. Where blue signals a Jedi Guardian, green a Jedi Consular, and red a Sith, orange occupies no official category. This absence of institutional affiliation is the point: orange lightsabers symbolize creativity, individuality, and a maverick spirit, Force users who walked a middle path between Jedi ideals of peace and Sith pragmatism, owing allegiance to neither orthodoxy.

The choice of Obi-Wan Kenobi's hilt design for the orange blade creates a deliberate tension: Obi-Wan is among the most orthodox Jedi in the saga, a rule-follower and tradition-keeper. Placing his hilt on a blade color that rejects Jedi orthodoxy suggests a relationship between form and function, tradition and rebellion, that is more nuanced than simple opposition. You can carry the tools of the old order without being bound by its categories. The background's visual interpretation of the Jester Universe connects the piece to the broader ecosystem of internet-native communities and their mythological frameworks.

The niqab (rendered in all white) is the piece's most culturally specific element, a garment with deep significance in Islamic tradition reimagined as a Jedi's robes. The choice to depict a Muslim woman as a Force-wielding warrior outside conventional alignment categories creates a layered commentary on the intersections between faith, agency, and the freedom to define your own relationship to institutional power.

## Visual Elements

The figure wears an all-white custom niqab, the traditional garment transformed into something between Jedi robes and personal armor. She holds an orange lightsaber with the distinctive hilt of Obi-Wan Kenobi's weapon, the orthodox form carrying the unorthodox blade. Her eyes are bright orange, matching the saber, creating a visual link between the wielder and the weapon that suggests the blade's color comes from her rather than from a kyber crystal. The background is a visual interpretation of the Jester Universe, grounding the piece in its community context. The composition is poised and deliberate, this is not a battle scene but a portrait, a figure who has chosen her stance and invites the viewer to understand it.

## Justification

Mijedi operates in moral gray areas, seeking balance and following an individual path rather than aligning with any single camp. The orange saber is a direct reflection of that: rare, deliberately outside the usual categories, and walking its own line.
