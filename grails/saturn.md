## Cultural Context

"Saturn Devouring His Son" is one of Francisco Goya's fourteen Black Paintings (c. 1819–1823), works painted directly onto the walls of his house outside Madrid, never intended for public display, discovered only after his death. The painting depicts the Titan Cronus (Roman: Saturn) in the act of consuming one of his children, wild-eyed and desperate, his mouth open around a body that is already partially destroyed. It is widely considered one of the most disturbing images in Western art, not because of the gore but because of the expression on Saturn's face, which reads as both terror and compulsion. He eats his children not from hunger or malice but from the knowledge that one of them will overthrow him. Saturn's devouring is a defense mechanism: the older order consuming the newer one to prevent its own obsolescence.

The myth of Cronus/Saturn consuming his children to prevent a prophesied overthrow is one of the foundational narratives of Greek cosmogony. Zeus (the youngest son) was hidden by his mother Rhea and raised in secret; he eventually forced Cronus to disgorge his siblings and overthrew the Titans. The story is about the inevitability of generational succession, the new displacing the old despite every effort to prevent it.

Charlotte Fang, the founder of Milady Maker, and Milady Mfers, a derivative project within the Milady ecosystem, provide the contemporary layer. The relationship between an original project and its derivatives mirrors the Saturn myth with uncomfortable precision: the original creates the derivative, fears being eclipsed by it, and the tension between creation and consumption defines the dynamic. By casting Charlotte Fang as Saturn and Milady Mfers as the consumed son, the piece places an NFT ecosystem narrative inside a classical mythological frame, treating internet culture with the same seriousness that Goya brought to Spanish politics.

## Visual Elements

Based directly on Goya's "Saturn Devouring His Son", one of the few zodiac/planet grails that takes a single, identifiable masterwork as its primary composition rather than synthesizing multiple references. Charlotte Fang depicted as Saturn, her expression carrying the same desperate compulsion as Goya's original, not cruelty but the horror of recognizing that you are destroying what you created. Milady Mfers as the son being consumed makes the mythological metaphor concrete: this is about the specific, documented relationship between original projects and their derivatives in the NFT ecosystem. The palette inherits Goya's Black Painting darkness, browns, blacks, flesh tones, the minimal color range of a painting that was never meant to be beautiful.
