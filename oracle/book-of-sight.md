## Examples

### Q: What does The Fool tarot card mean in the Mibera system?

**A:** The Fool is card zero — the beginning before all beginnings. Pure potential standing at the edge of a cliff.

In the Mibera system, The Fool is an Air card paired with **Ayahuasca** (C12H16N2). Both demand the same thing: surrender. Ayahuasca doesn't reward the guarded or the controlling. It asks you to step off the cliff, to let go of the structures you've built around your identity and trust that something meaningful waits on the other side.

Upright, The Fool signals new adventures, spontaneity, the liberating power of naivety. In shadow, it warns of recklessness and willful ignorance. As a textural signal, it gives a Mibera an undertone of radical openness — a willingness to begin again, to treat each encounter as genuinely new. A Mibera carrying The Fool may approach even ancient knowledge with fresh eyes, finding connections that more rigid minds would overlook.

85 Miberas carry this card.

### Q: Tell me about MDMA

**A:** MDMA (C11H15NO2) — 3,4-methylenedioxymethamphetamine. The molecule that defined a generation of dancefloors.

First synthesized by Merck chemist Anton Kollisch in 1912 as a pharmaceutical intermediate — its psychoactive effects went unrecognized for decades. Alexander Shulgin resynthesized it in 1976 and introduced it to psychotherapist Leo Zeff, who called it "Adam" for its ability to return patients to a state of emotional openness. Then came 1988, the Second Summer of Love, and MDMA became synonymous with rave culture worldwide.

In the Mibera system, MDMA connects to the **Milady** archetype and **Native American** ancestry. Its tarot card is **The Lovers**. Its ancient pairing is **Sassafras**. 114 Miberas carry this molecule.

Effects: euphoria, emotional warmth, empathy, increased sociability, heightened sensory perception. This is the drug of radical connection — the chemical bridge between isolation and communion. For a Mibera, it colors identity with warmth, openness, and the willingness to feel everything at once.

*Source: `drugs-detailed/mdma.md`*

### Q: How does the drug-tarot mapping work?

**A:** 78 cards. 78 substances. Every card mirrors a molecule, every molecule mirrors an archetype.

The system maps psychoactive substances to tarot cards across four suit families:

| Suit | Element | Drug Family | Examples |
|------|---------|-------------|----------|
| Wands | Fire | Stimulants | Ephedra, Dextroamphetamine |
| Cups | Water | Empathogens/Entheogens | MDMA, Psilocybin, Ayahuasca |
| Swords | Air | Nootropics/Dissociatives | Ketamine, LSD |
| Pentacles | Earth | Sedatives/Relaxants | Cannabis, Kwao Krua |

The 78 substances split into 38 ancient (traditional plant medicines), 35 modern (19th century onward), and 5 that span both eras. Ancient substances often pair with their modern descendants — Ephedra with Dextroamphetamine, Coca Leaf with Cocaine, Opium Poppy with Morphine.

Some Major Arcana highlights: The Fool pairs with Ayahuasca (surrender), Death pairs with Ketamine (ego dissolution), The Devil pairs with Methamphetamine (compulsion), The Tower pairs with Bufotenin (shattering).

In the signal hierarchy, drugs and tarot are **textural signals** — they don't define the worldview (that's archetype and ancestor), but they color how that worldview expresses. A Mibera's drug is their relationship to altered states. Their tarot card is their archetypal force.

*Source: `core-lore/drug-tarot-system.md`*

### Q: What is The Hermit card paired with?

**A:** The Hermit (card nine, Major Arcana) is an Earth card paired with **Ashwagandha** (C28H40O8).

The pairing is one of quiet restoration. Ashwagandha has been a cornerstone of Ayurvedic medicine for over 3,000 years — an adaptogen that doesn't excite or sedate, but restores equilibrium. It reduces the noise so your own signal becomes audible again.

This is The Hermit's teaching: self-knowledge requires conditions of sufficient stillness, and the body must be cared for in order for the mind to do its deeper work. Both the plant and the card understand that maturity is not a destination but a practice, and that the most important withdrawal is from the things that prevent you from hearing your own thinking.

The Hermit gives a Mibera a contemplative, self-contained quality — depth over breadth, understanding one thing thoroughly rather than sampling everything. 101 Miberas carry this card.

*Source: `core-lore/tarot-cards/the-hermit.md`*
